About

An ecological footprint, as defined by the Oxford Dictionary online, is “the impact of a person or community on the environment, expressed as the amount of land required to sustain their use of natural resources.” The result is a measurement of land and water area needed to replace the resources used (measured in global hectares).

A carbon footprint, as defined by the Oxford Dictionary online, is “The amount of carbon dioxideand other carbon compounds emitted due to the consumption of fossil fuels by a particularperson, group, etc.” The result is a measurement of the greenhouse gases (usually in tonnes of carbon dioxide) being emitted by the user. Both the ecological footprint and the carbon footprint measure the amount of resources we use.
